# Literature Map

This map records what each source supports for the selfhood research program. A source can support a mechanism without proving consciousness or proving that selves are real.

## Control and Regulation

### Good Regulator Theorem

Source: Conant and Ashby, "Every good regulator of a system must be a model of that system" (1970). DOI: [10.1080/00207727008920220](https://doi.org/10.1080/00207727008920220). Overview with abstract: [EA Pad](https://eapad.dk/article/every-good-regulator-of-a-system-must-be-a-model-of-that-system/).

Use: supports the general claim that successful, simple regulation requires model structure.

Limit: it does not by itself prove selfhood. The bridge is conditional: if the regulated system includes the agent's own body, internal needs, sensors, or actuators, then a good regulator needs a model of those agent variables too.

## Partial Observability and Predictive State

### Predictive State Representations

Source: Littman and Sutton, "Predictive Representations of State" (NeurIPS 2001): [paper page](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/1983-predictive-representations-of-state).

Use: shows that state can be represented as action-conditional predictions of future observations, not necessarily as hand-labeled latent objects. This is important because a self may emerge as a predictive state rather than a symbolic self-object.

Limit: supports the optional/illusion hypothesis too. If prediction-only state representations solve the tasks, explicit selfhood is not necessary.

### RL2 and Recurrent Meta-RL

Source: Duan et al., "RL^2: Fast Reinforcement Learning via Slow Reinforcement Learning" (2016): [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1611.02779). Related neuroscience framing: Wang et al., "Prefrontal cortex as a meta-reinforcement learning system" (Nature Neuroscience 2018): [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41593-018-0147-8).

Use: recurrent activations can store task/adaptation state across episodes. This gives a serious alternative to explicit self models.

Limit: if self-equivalent information is hidden in recurrent state, the right question becomes whether it is decodable and causally useful, not whether a named "self" module exists.

## Self-Modeling Robots

### Continuous Self-Modeling After Damage

Source: Bongard, Zykov, and Lipson, "Resilient machines through continuous self-modeling" (Science 2006): [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17110570/), DOI [10.1126/science.1133687](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1133687).

Use: direct evidence that a robot can infer its own structure from actuation-sensation relationships and use that model for locomotion recovery after damage.

Limit: this is self-modeling for body/control, not evidence of subjective experience.

### Task-Agnostic Self-Modeling

Source: Kwiatkowski and Lipson, "Task-agnostic self-modeling machines" (Science Robotics 2019): [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33137761/), DOI [10.1126/scirobotics.aau9354](https://doi.org/10.1126/scirobotics.aau9354).

Use: supports the claim that self-models can be learned without prior knowledge of physics or shape and then reused for tasks and damage detection.

Limit: still a body-model result, not a general theory of identity or consciousness.

### Full-Body Visual Self-Modeling

Source: Chen et al., "Full-Body Visual Self-Modeling of Robot Morphologies" (Science Robotics 2022): [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.06389), DOI [10.1126/scirobotics.abn1944](https://doi.org/10.1126/scirobotics.abn1944).

Use: strengthens the robotics path by modeling body occupancy and morphology from interaction data.

Limit: visual body self-modeling may not generalize to symbolic, social, or narrative selfhood.

## Error Attribution and Agency

### Corollary Discharge and Reafference

Source: Crapse and Sommer, "Corollary discharge across the animal kingdom" (Nature Reviews Neuroscience 2008): [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5153363/).

Use: supports the mechanism that mobile agents need to distinguish self-generated sensory changes from externally generated changes.

Limit: this can operate without reflective selfhood or consciousness.

### Forward Models and Awareness of Action

Sources:

- Wolpert, Ghahramani, and Jordan, "An internal model for sensorimotor integration" (Science 1995): DOI [10.1126/science.7569931](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.7569931).
- Frith, Blakemore, and Wolpert, "Abnormalities in the awareness and control of action" (Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B 2000): [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1692910/), DOI [10.1098/rstb.2000.0734](https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2000.0734).
- Blakemore, Wolpert, and Frith, "Abnormalities in the awareness of action" (Trends in Cognitive Sciences 2002): DOI [10.1016/S1364-6613(02)01907-1](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1364-6613(02)01907-1).

Use: supports the comparator/error-attribution pathway from motor command to predicted sensory outcome to agency.

Limit: agency attribution is not the same thing as persistent identity.

## Internal State, Survival, and Value

### Homeostatic Reinforcement Learning

Source: Keramati and Gutkin, "Homeostatic reinforcement learning for integrating reward collection and physiological stability" (eLife 2014): [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4270100/), DOI [10.7554/eLife.04811](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.04811).

Use: supports the claim that internal physiological variables can define value and action selection. This is central for self as control variable.

Limit: homeostatic control can be minimal and non-conscious.

### Instrumental Power and Self-Preservation

Source: Turner et al., "Optimal Policies Tend To Seek Power" (NeurIPS 2021): [NeurIPS](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2021/hash/c26820b8a4c1b3c2aa868d6d57e14a79-Abstract.html).

Use: supports a formal version of counterfactual self-preservation as option preservation under many reward functions.

Limit: power-seeking is not selfhood. It is relevant only where maintaining the agent's future ability to act is instrumentally valuable.

## Active Inference, Boundary, and Self-Evidencing

### Free Energy Principle

Source: Friston, "The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?" (Nature Reviews Neuroscience 2010): [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrn2787), DOI [10.1038/nrn2787](https://doi.org/10.1038/nrn2787).

Use: provides a broad predictive-control frame: action, perception, and learning as prediction-error/free-energy minimization.

Limit: the framework is broad and can overgeneralize. It should generate experiments, not settle them.

### Markov Blankets of Life

Source: Kirchhoff et al., "The Markov blankets of life: autonomy, active inference and the free energy principle" (2018): [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5805980/), DOI [10.1098/rsif.2017.0792](https://doi.org/10.1098/rsif.2017.0792).

Use: gives a formal vocabulary for boundaries between internal states, sensory states, active states, and external states.

Limit: a Markov blanket is not automatically a conscious self. It is a boundary condition for autonomous inference/control.

## Embodiment and Body Ownership

### Interoceptive Inference

Source: Seth, "Interoceptive inference, emotion, and the embodied self" (Trends in Cognitive Sciences 2013): [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24126130/), DOI [10.1016/j.tics.2013.09.007](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2013.09.007).

Use: supports the idea that predictive models of internal bodily signals contribute to embodied selfhood.

Limit: primarily a human/neuroscience theory, not direct proof that artificial agents need selves.

### Minimal Self and Illusions

Sources:

- Botvinick and Cohen, "Rubber hands feel touch that eyes see" (Nature 1998): DOI [10.1038/35784](https://doi.org/10.1038/35784).
- Blanke and Metzinger, "Full-body illusions and minimal phenomenal selfhood" (Trends in Cognitive Sciences 2009): DOI [10.1016/j.tics.2008.10.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2008.10.003).
- Gallagher, "Philosophical conceptions of the self: implications for cognitive science" (Trends in Cognitive Sciences 2000): DOI [10.1016/S1364-6613(99)01417-5](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1364-6613(99)01417-5).
- Metzinger, "Being No One: The Self-Model Theory of Subjectivity" (MIT Press 2003): [MIT Press](https://direct.mit.edu/books/monograph/1991/Being-No-OneThe-Self-Model-Theory-of-Subjectivity).

Use: separates minimal embodied self, narrative self, and self-as-model. Helps avoid treating "self" as one thing.

Limit: philosophical and human phenomenological claims must be operationalized before they can test artificial agents.

## Consciousness Layer: Keep Separate For Now

### Global Neuronal Workspace

Source: Dehaene and Naccache, "Towards a cognitive neuroscience of consciousness: basic evidence and a workspace framework" (Cognition 2001): [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11164022/), DOI [10.1016/S0010-0277(00)00123-2](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0010-0277(00)00123-2).

Use: relevant to global availability, multimodal binding, and reportability.

Limit: conscious access is not the same question as why a persistent self exists.

### Integrated Information Theory

Source: Tononi, "An information integration theory of consciousness" (BMC Neuroscience 2004): [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/1471-2202-5-42), DOI [10.1186/1471-2202-5-42](https://doi.org/10.1186/1471-2202-5-42).

Use: relevant to integration and unity claims in the phenomenology appendix.

Limit: should not be used as a shortcut proof of selfhood or artificial consciousness.
