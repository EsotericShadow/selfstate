# Phenomenology To Model Variables

The author's first-person observations are treated as phenomenological data and hypothesis generators. They are not evidence that any theory of selfhood or consciousness is true.

## Mapping Observations To Candidate Mechanisms

### Unified Experiential Plane

Observation: imagery, language, memory, emotion, sound, body sensation, and imagined futures appear within one coherent frame.

Candidate mechanism:

- multimodal binding;
- global availability;
- shared latent workspace;
- attention-mediated integration.

Experimental translation:

- test whether globally shared state improves cross-modal prediction and action relative to isolated modules.

### Spatial Simulation

Observation: internal objects can be rotated, scaled, transformed, inspected, and viewed from multiple perspectives.

Candidate mechanism:

- generative spatial world model;
- object-centric latent representation;
- counterfactual simulation;
- viewpoint transformation.

Experimental translation:

- test whether spatial latent simulation improves planning, body adaptation, and perspective shifts.

### Multimodal Integration

Observation: visual, auditory, emotional, bodily, and conceptual material can coexist.

Candidate mechanism:

- cross-modal predictive coding;
- common indexing of objects/events across modalities;
- coherence pressure across modality-specific predictions.

Experimental translation:

- introduce modality conflicts and measure whether agents form unified event/self representations.

### Temporal Manipulation

Observation: past events can be replayed, futures simulated, time compressed, and alternatives branched.

Candidate mechanism:

- episodic memory;
- model-based planning;
- temporal abstraction;
- counterfactual rollout.

Experimental translation:

- test whether future self-state rollouts improve delayed reward, interruption recovery, and damage planning.

### Persistent Observer

Observation: a stable observing perspective seems to persist through changing content.

Candidate mechanism:

- self-index;
- continuity binding;
- perspective-centered representation;
- stable reference frame for memory and planning.

Experimental translation:

- test whether a persistent self-index improves memory retrieval, plan continuation, and cross-context consistency.

### Recursive Self-Inspection

Observation: thoughts, thoughts about thoughts, and models of models can become objects of attention.

Candidate mechanism:

- metacognition;
- introspective access to internal state;
- uncertainty monitoring;
- meta-policy evaluation.

Experimental translation:

- test whether agents that model their own uncertainty, competence, and policy limitations seek information more effectively.

### Symbolic-Spatial Translation

Observation: abstract concepts often appear as spatial structures.

Candidate mechanism:

- shared representational substrate for relational reasoning;
- graph-to-space embeddings;
- object-file representations for abstract entities.

Experimental translation:

- compare symbolic-only, spatial-only, and hybrid latent representations on causal reasoning and planning tasks.

### Object Persistence

Observation: objects continue to exist latently when not actively attended.

Candidate mechanism:

- object permanence;
- latent state maintenance;
- generative memory.

Experimental translation:

- test whether latent object persistence improves planning under occlusion and delayed observation.

### Identity Continuity

Observation: past self, present self, and future self are experienced as one continuous process.

Candidate mechanism:

- process identity rather than object identity;
- memory-linked agent-state trajectory;
- commitment tracking;
- autobiographical compression.

Experimental translation:

- test whether continuity variables improve long-horizon commitments and prevent incoherent goal resets.

### Coherence Pressure

Observation: contradiction generates discomfort and further processing; understanding feels intrinsically rewarding.

Candidate mechanism:

- prediction-error minimization;
- uncertainty reduction;
- conflict monitoring;
- intrinsic reward for model improvement.

Experimental translation:

- compare agents with and without intrinsic coherence/error-reduction objectives under contradiction and incomplete information.

### Subjective Continuity

Observation: experience appears as a continuous stream despite changing content, memories, and beliefs.

Candidate mechanism:

- unresolved;
- possible reconstruction from memory and coherence maintenance;
- possible consequence of global workspace dynamics;
- possible consequence of predictive temporal smoothing;
- possible phenomenon outside the current selfhood layer.

Experimental translation:

- do not assume this has been explained. Treat it as a target phenomenon only after world-model, self-model, coherence, identity, and first-person frame have been separately tested.

## Guardrail

The appendix should never be used as:

- proof that consciousness exists in any system;
- proof that selfhood is real rather than constructed;
- proof that a global workspace is necessary;
- evidence that artificial systems with similar mechanisms are conscious.

It is useful because it supplies candidate features that a later model may need to explain.
